    • The easiest form to transfer ownership is a corporation.

    • In a corporation, ownership is represented by shares of stock, which can be easily bought and sold without affecting the operations or legal status of the company. This makes it straightforward for shareholders to transfer their ownership interest to others.

    • On the other hand, a sole proprietorship is owned and operated by a single individual. Transferring ownership in a sole proprietorship involves selling the entire business, including its assets and liabilities, which can be more complicated and requires more legal and administrative work.

    • Therefore, in terms of ease of transferring ownership, a corporation is the better option.
